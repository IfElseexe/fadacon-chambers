Privacy Policy

How Fadacon and CG Chambers handles your personal information. Last updated: July 2026.

1. Who we are

Fadacon and CG Chambers, Counsel & Advocates ("the Chambers", "we"), RC No. 7965778, of C22, PEB 04 Plaza, Plot 2027 Dalaba Street, Wuse Zone 5, FCT Abuja, is committed to protecting your privacy in line with the Nigeria Data Protection Act and our professional obligations of confidentiality as legal practitioners.

2. Information we collect

  • Enquiry information, when you use our consultation form, we receive the details you provide: your name, phone number, email address (if given), the type of matter, and your description of the issue.
  • Communications, records of your correspondence with us by phone, WhatsApp or email.

We do not use tracking or advertising cookies on this website.

3. How we use your information

We use the information you provide solely to respond to your enquiry, to advise and represent you where we accept your instructions, to maintain our professional records, and to comply with legal and regulatory obligations. We do not sell your information or use it for marketing.

4. How enquiries are delivered

Enquiries submitted through the website form are transmitted to the Chambers' email via a form-delivery service. By submitting the form, you consent to this transmission. Please avoid including highly sensitive details in your first enquiry.

5. Confidentiality

All information you share with the Chambers is treated in strict professional confidence. Where a solicitor–client relationship is established, your information is additionally protected by legal professional privilege to the extent the law provides.

6. Storage and retention

We retain enquiry and client information only for as long as necessary for the purposes described above, and in line with the retention obligations applicable to legal practitioners in Nigeria.

7. Disclosure

We do not disclose your information to third parties except where necessary to handle your matter with your instructions, or where required by law or a court of competent jurisdiction.

8. Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal information held by the Chambers by contacting us using the details below.

9. Contact

For any privacy questions or requests, contact the Chambers at imeguchristopher@gmail.com or 0706 341 5883.
