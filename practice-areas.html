Criminal Practice

The consequences of a criminal allegation are among the most serious a person can face — liberty, livelihood and reputation are all at stake. Our Chambers provides vigorous and principled representation to persons under investigation, standing trial, or seeking to protect their rights within the criminal justice system.

We appear before Magistrates' Courts, High Courts and appellate courts across Nigeria, handling matters from bail applications and police invitations through to full trials and appeals. Our approach combines meticulous case preparation with firm advocacy: we scrutinise the prosecution's evidence, protect our clients' constitutional rights at every stage, and pursue the earliest and best possible resolution — whether that is a discharge, an acquittal, or a favourable negotiated outcome.

We also advise complainants and victims of crime on their options, including private prosecutions and petitions to the appropriate authorities. Whatever side of a criminal matter you are on, early legal advice makes a decisive difference. Do not make any statement or sign any document before speaking to counsel.

Civil Litigation

Disputes are a reality of business and personal life — over contracts, land, debts, employment, defamation, tenancy and more. When a dispute cannot be resolved amicably, our Chambers provides effective and determined representation before all courts of record in Nigeria.

Litigation at Fadacon and CG Chambers begins long before the courtroom. We assess the strength of your case candidly, advise on the costs and realistic timelines involved, and design a strategy aimed at your commercial or personal objective — not litigation for its own sake. Where an early settlement serves you better, we will tell you so and negotiate from a position of strength.

Our civil practice covers pre-action advice and demand letters, originating processes and pleadings, interlocutory applications and injunctions, trials, enforcement of judgments, and appeals. Throughout, you receive regular reports in plain language, so you always know exactly where your matter stands.

Military Law

Military law is a specialised field that very few chambers in Nigeria handle with genuine depth. It is governed by its own statutes, procedures and traditions — principally the Armed Forces Act — and demands counsel who understand both the letter of military law and the realities of service life.

Our Chambers advises and represents officers, ratings and other ranks in matters including courts-martial and summary trials, boards of inquiry, redress of grievance procedures, matters of discipline and administrative action, terminal benefits and pension entitlements, and the interface between military service and civil rights.

We also act for civilians and businesses whose matters intersect with military institutions, and for families of service personnel. Proceedings within the military justice system move quickly and carry serious career consequences; engaging experienced counsel at the earliest possible stage is essential to protecting your position.

Admiralty Law

Admiralty and maritime law governs commerce and conduct on Nigeria's waterways and territorial waters — a body of law with its own jurisdiction, vested principally in the Federal High Court. It touches shipping, carriage of goods by sea, marine insurance, and the rights and liabilities of vessel owners, charterers, cargo interests and seafarers.

Our admiralty practice includes claims for cargo loss or damage, charterparty and bill of lading disputes, ship arrest and release proceedings, collision and salvage matters, marine insurance claims, and seafarers' employment and injury claims. We also advise businesses in the maritime and logistics sector on their contracts and regulatory obligations.

Maritime claims are often time-sensitive — vessels move, cargo perishes and limitation periods run. If your matter touches the sea or inland waterways, contact the Chambers promptly so that your rights can be preserved.

Corporate & Real Property

Sound legal structure is the foundation of every successful business and every secure property holding. Our corporate and real property practice serves entrepreneurs, companies, families and investors at every stage — from formation and acquisition through growth, restructuring and succession.

On the corporate side, we handle company incorporation and post-incorporation matters at the Corporate Affairs Commission, shareholders' and partnership agreements, commercial contracts, regulatory compliance, and corporate advisory. We pride ourselves on drafting agreements that anticipate disputes before they arise.

On the property side, we advise on acquisitions and disposals of land and developed property, verification of title and due diligence, leases and tenancy matters, mortgages and secured lending, and disputes over ownership and boundaries. In a market where defective titles and fraudulent transactions are common, thorough legal due diligence before you pay is the cheapest insurance you will ever buy.

Conveyancing & Change of Ownership

Conveyancing is the legal transfer of interest in land or property from one party to another — and it is where many Nigerians suffer avoidable losses through incomplete documentation or unperfected titles. Our Chambers manages the entire process so that what you buy is truly, legally yours.

Our conveyancing services include preparation and review of deeds of assignment, contracts of sale and land agreements; searches and investigations of title at the relevant land registries; obtaining governor's consent; stamping and registration of instruments; and perfection of title. We also handle change of ownership and regularisation of documentation for property inherited, gifted or purchased informally.

Whether you are buying your first plot, formalising a family property, or completing a commercial acquisition, we ensure every document is properly executed, every consent obtained, and every registration completed — with clear guidance on official fees at each step.

Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)

Not every dispute belongs in a courtroom. Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) — negotiation, mediation, conciliation and arbitration — can resolve conflicts faster, more privately and often at far lower cost than litigation, while preserving valuable business and family relationships.

Our Chambers represents parties in arbitration proceedings, acts in mediations and conciliations, and advises on the drafting and enforcement of dispute resolution clauses in contracts. We also assist with the enforcement of arbitral awards and negotiated settlements, converting agreements into binding, executable outcomes.

Consistent with our motto — service for humanity — we always assess whether an amicable resolution can achieve your objectives before recommending litigation. Where ADR is the wiser path, we pursue it skilfully; where firmness in court is required, we do not hesitate.
